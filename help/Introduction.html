What is it?

RobotBuilder is an application designed to aid the robot development process. Robot builder can help you:

  • Generating boilerplate code.
  • Organize your robot and figure out what its key subsystems are.
  • Check that you have enough channels for all of your sensors and actuators.
  • Generate wiring diagrams.
  • Easily modify your operator interface.
  • More...

Getting Started

This section will take you through the basics of using RobotBuilder to develop a simple robot with tank drive. We will cover:

  • Subsystems: The physical parts of your robot.
  • Commands: The actions your robot can perform.
  • Operator Interface: How your drivers interact with the robot.
  • Generating boilerplate code.
  • Generating wiring diagrams.

Subsystems

Subsystems are RobotBuilders way of organizing your robot. A subsystem should represent a distinct part of the robot that performs a particular subset of tasks. For example, a Drive Train consists of a series of motors that drive it and maybe some encoders to measure it's speed and distance. For more, see: Subsystems and Subsystem

Creating the Drive Train

The first step on the way to a fully functional subsystem is to create the subsystem, so that it exists in your robot tree.

To create the Drive Train subsystem:

  • Click on "Subsystem" in the palette on the left.
  • Drag it over Subsystems in the robot tree and release.

    • A new subystem has been created.

Naming the Drive Train

After you have a subsytem created, it is very helpful to give it a name.

To name the subsystem:

  • Click on the newly created "Subsystem 1".

    • It's properties will appear on the right.

  • Double click the cell to the right of name, that contains "Subsystem 1".
  • Edit it to read "Drive Train".

    • It's new name appears in the tree.

Adding a Robot Drive to the Drive Train

The easiest way to make your robot drive is to us the RobotDrive object in WPILib. It provides methods for tank drive, arcade drive and mechanum drive.

To add a robot drive to your Drive Train:

  • Click on "Robot Drive 2" in the palette on the left.
  • Drag it over "Drive Train" in the robot tree and release.

    • Your Drive Train now has a robot drive object.

  • Rename it to "Robot Drive", the same way you renamed the "Drive Train".

Adding Motors to the Robot Drive

The RobotDrive object needs motors for bothi it's left and and it's right side.

To add motors to your robot drive:

  • Click on "Jaguar" in the palette on the left.
  • Drag it over "Robot Drive" in the robot tree and release.
  • Rename it to "Left Jaguar", the same way you renamed the "Drive Train".
  • Click on "Jaguar" in the palette on the left.
  • Drag it over "Robot Drive" in the robot tree and release.
  • Rename it to "Right Jaguar", the same way you renamed the "Drive Train".

Checking That the Motors are Correctly Assigned

The RobotDrive object tries to guess which motors are which, but it's helpful to check that they are correctly assigned to the proper side.

To add motors to your robot drive:

  • Click on "Robot Drive".
  • Make sure that the "Left Motor" property is set to "Left Jaguar", if not click on it and select "Left Jaguar".
  • Make sure that the "Right Motor" property is set to "Right Jaguar", if not click on it and select "Right Jaguar".

You now have a subsystem representing your robots Drive Train!
